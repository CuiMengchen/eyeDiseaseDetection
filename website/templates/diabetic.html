Diabetic retinopathy

Disease Description

Diabetic retinopathy (DR) is one of the most common microvascular complications of diabetes. It is the leakage and obstruction of retinal microvessels caused by chronic progressive diabetes, which causes a series of fundus lesions, such as microvascular tumors, hard exudates, cotton wool spots, neonatal Blood vessels, vitreous proliferation, macular edema and even retinal detachment. DR can be divided into proliferative diabetic retinopathy and non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy based on the presence or absence of abnormal new blood vessels emanating from the retina.

Check item

1. Visual acuity test, intraocular pressure test, gonioscopy if necessary.

2. Fundus photography after mydriasis and fundus examination under slit lamp.

3. Fundus photography to record the severity of diabetes.

4. Optical coherence tomography (OCT detection).

5. Fluorescein angiography (FFA examination).

Diagnose

Diabetic retinopathy is one of the highly specific chronic complications of diabetes, but diabetic patients are also at high risk of early onset of other eye diseases, including cataract, retinal vascular occlusion, and ischemic optic neuropathy. Therefore, it is recommended that patients with diabetes undergo the first comprehensive eye examination in the ophthalmology department. The examination items mainly include visual acuity, blood pressure, anterior chamber angle, iris, crystal and fundus examination. Doctors will conduct detailed examinations from multiple aspects to make differential judgments.

Treatment measures

1. Panretinal photocoagulation (PRP) treatment Fundus laser treatment is an effective means to prevent blindness in diabetic patients. Clinically, PRP is still the diabetic retina after the severe non-proliferative stage. An effective method for the regression of membrane lesions. Fundus laser treatment is less painful, convenient, and economical. Every diabetic patient should correctly understand the function of fundus laser treatment, and conduct regular eye examinations with dilated pupils. At the end of the day, timely detection of diabetic retinopathy that damages eyesight and timely laser treatment to ensure a good quality of life.

2. Focal photocoagulation (Focal) treatment of the macula Focal photocoagulation of the macula is an effective method for the treatment of focal macular scuba lung, and it is also a complementary treatment to anti-vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) therapy. Regular reexamination is necessary after fundus laser treatment. The purpose is to know whether the space and range of retinal photocoagulation are sufficient, whether the fundus lesions have improved and Whether supplementary laser treatment is needed, etc.
